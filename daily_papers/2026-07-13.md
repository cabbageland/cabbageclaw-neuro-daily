It earns archive status because it does not stop at the old "heterogeneity matters" slogan. It fits arbitrary empirical parameter distributions with a Lorentzian mixture, turns each component into one reduced ensemble, and then uses the resulting low-dimensional system to recover macroscopic dynamics and bifurcation structure that would usually be available only for mathematically convenient disorder distributions. ## Most relevant paper

**A multi-ensemble mean-field reduction method for networks of globally coupled phase oscillators with arbitrary parameter distributions**

This gets the top rank because it gives a concrete answer to a recurring modeling failure mode. A lot of neuroscience papers want three things at once: biological heterogeneity, low-dimensional interpretability, and analytically usable dynamics. Usually they keep two and fake the third. This paper is one of the better attempts to keep all three by approximating ugly empirical parameter distributions with a Lorentzian mixture whose components each inherit a tractable mean-field description.

The useful thing is not just the fitting trick. The authors show that distribution shape changes the regime story. In the neural section, empirically measured mouse cortical cell-property distributions lead to different synchronization transitions and multistability structure across cell types and cortical layers. That is much closer to the actual scientific question than collapsing heterogeneity to one variance parameter and declaring the problem solved.

The comparison with today's other fresh papers is why it stayed on top. The reward-pursuit rTMS paper is worthwhile because it tries to operationalize anhedonia with a translational task rather than with vibes alone. But it is still a small naturalistic correlation study. HemoPIC is technically serious and more mechanistic than a lot of "digital twin" branding, but its perfusion-stroke focus does not transfer as directly to cabbageland's intervention logic. The fresh MBCT and mindful-hypnotherapy papers are clinically interesting, but they are not doing the kind of state-estimation, targeting, or regime-control work that would justify a preserve over today's heterogeneity paper.

The caveats are real. The reduction only applies to a restricted class of globally coupled phase-oscillator and QIF-style systems, and the neural examples are still simplified population models rather than anatomically realistic cortical circuits. But those are normal modeling limits, not decorative overclaim. The paper earns keep status because it gives a better language for linking empirical heterogeneity to the dynamical regimes that interventions would actually have to steer.

## Novelty / framing / baseline impact

- **For mechanistic neuroscience:** heterogeneity should be treated as a structured object that can create or destroy synchrony and multistability, not as a nuisance width parameter.
- **For low-dimensional model design:** distributional realism and tractability do not have to be mutually exclusive if the fitted representation is chosen for downstream analysis rather than mere density aesthetics.
- **For digital-twin rhetoric:** today's preserve and yesterday's viable-parameter-manifold paper form a useful pair. One says the space of compatible parameters matters; the other says the shape of heterogeneity inside that space matters too.
- **For translational neuromodulation:** the fresh reward-pursuit rTMS paper is interesting because it ties a behavioral task to anhedonia change, but the small naturalistic sample keeps it below preserve threshold.
- **For methods / neuroengineering:** HemoPIC looks like one of the better recent "digital twin" papers because it actually solves a constrained inverse problem and produces executable perfusion dynamics, but it is still more stroke-perfusion infrastructure than intervention logic for this archive.
- **For CBT plus interventional psychiatry:** the fresh gambling-disorder rTMS-plus-CBT RCT remains a reminder that combination-treatment enthusiasm still outruns transferability and mechanistic clarity.
- **For hypnosis and hypnotherapy:** the fresh mindful hypnotherapy RCT suggests clinical signal in a personality-disorder setting, but it is still mechanism-thin compared with the existing hypnosis state-structure anchor.

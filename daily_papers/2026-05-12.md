## Short overview

The most relevant paper today is **A visual cortex origin of trauma memory intrusions: integrated evidence from TMS and fMRI**. Using a trauma-film paradigm in healthy participants, the authors combined functional MRI with one-hertz repetitive transcranial magnetic stimulation to early visual cortex versus vertex control. They report reduced intrusion frequency, vividness, and emotional intensity over the next seven days after visual-cortex stimulation, while recognition of episodic gist was preserved. The mechanistic claim is that intrusive episodes are tracked more tightly by middle-frontal-gyrus to occipital-visual-cortex interactions than by the usual limbic-prefrontal story. If that result is solid, it is a more intervention-usable framing of intrusive memory than generic PTSD network rhetoric.

The second paper is **Altered flow of information in attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder**. In a reasonably sized developmental sample of 355 children, the authors estimate directed information flow using transfer entropy and report two opposing ADHD-related patterns: decreased flow converging on right visual cortex and right ventral anterior thalamus, and increased flow converging on right frontal operculum. The paper is useful mainly as a network-directionality paper, not as a biomarker solution. It sharpens the question of where information-routing bottlenecks may sit in ADHD, but it does not yet show that these measures are stable enough or intervention-linked enough to guide treatment.

The third paper is **Acoustic Coupling for Double-Blind Human Low-Intensity Focused Ultrasound Neuromodulation**. This is not glamorous, but it is worth preserving because the field badly needs credible sham infrastructure. The authors build gel-plastic coupling devices that are visually and tactually matched while either transmitting ultrasound efficiently or blocking it strongly through an internal air gap. At 0.50 megahertz, their preferred verum configuration shows low insertion loss and minimal beam distortion, while the sham shows roughly 31 decibels of insertion loss. That is exactly the kind of unsexy methods paper that makes future causal claims less embarrassing.

## Most relevant paper

**A visual cortex origin of trauma memory intrusions: integrated evidence from TMS and fMRI**

This gets top rank because it carries actual intervention logic. It does not just say visual cortex lights up around intrusions. It perturbs early visual cortex, tracks downstream effects on intrusion phenomenology, and uses connectivity analyses to argue that frontal-visual interactions matter more than a purely limbic interpretation for this specific symptom class.

## Novelty / framing / baseline impact

- **For PTSD neuromodulation framing:** the trauma-intrusions paper argues that sensory cortex can be a causal node in intrusive memory formation, not merely a downstream readout. That could widen target logic beyond standard prefrontal control sites.
- **For developmental network framing:** the ADHD transfer-entropy paper is useful because it adds directionality and convergence structure, though it remains closer to mechanistic hypothesis generation than actionable targeting.
- **For ultrasound neuromodulation rigor:** the LIFU coupling paper is a reminder that sham engineering is part of the science. A field with weak blinding cannot make strong causal claims.
- **Adjacent but not preserved today:** **Differential modulation of sensorimotor beta oscillations by beta tACS alone and combined repetitive paired-pulse TMS with tACS** is mechanistically interesting, especially the separation between cortico-muscular coherence and resting-beta effects, but it did not beat the three papers above on immediate transfer value.

## One-paragraph takeaway

The best papers today either sharpen causal target logic or improve the credibility of future claims. The trauma-memory paper is the standout because it ties symptom change to a concrete perturbation target and a specific frontal-visual interaction story. The ADHD paper is useful because it treats directionality, not just connectivity strength, as the object of explanation. And the ultrasound paper matters because rigorous sham design is still one of the field's bottlenecks. Better control conditions are not decorative methods. They are part of whether the rest of the literature deserves belief.

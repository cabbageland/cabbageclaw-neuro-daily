## Short overview

The new preserve today is the sEEG-guided DBS workflow paper. It matters because psychiatric and pain-focused DBS still suffers from an awkward bottleneck that many reviews talk around: chronic implants cover only a handful of sites, revision surgeries are costly, and a lot of target selection remains inherited from group-average anatomy or favorite-circuit rhetoric. This paper does something more operationally serious. It uses temporary inpatient stereoelectroencephalography, sham-controlled symptom testing, and explicit power logic to decide which sites deserve chronic implantation. The useful result is not a single magical target. It is the argument that target search itself needs a discipline. In their data, sham variability strongly predicts stimulation-response variability, about ten sham trials are needed to estimate that variability robustly, and roughly ten trials per site are enough when the effect size is large enough to matter clinically.

The best way to read that note is alongside four existing preserves. **Conserved fiber topography of the anterior limb of the internal capsule in treatment-resistant psychiatric patients** remains the white-matter prerequisite: if route geometry is not preserved, personalized targeting claims are already in trouble. **Target engagement in a head-to-head clinical trial of dysphoric versus anxiosomatic transcranial magnetic stimulation targets** remains the better clinical warning that baseline site-to-circuit suitability may matter more than post-treatment connectivity theater. **Learning Temporal Basis Vectors for Closed-Loop Neural Stimulation** remains the right computational-control companion because it asks what kind of response model is efficient enough to actually support adaptive stimulation. And **Noninvasive brain stimulation combined with evidence-based psychotherapy for psychiatric disorders: A meta-analysis of optimal implementation parameters** remains the best standing-interest context for CBT-plus-interventional-psychiatry design, because it narrows timing and fidelity questions without pretending that generic synergy talk is mechanism.

## Most relevant paper

**Toward Individualized Deep Brain Stimulation: A Stereoelectroencephalography-Based Workflow for Neurostimulation Target Identification**

This gets top rank because it addresses a real intervention bottleneck that a lot of neuromodulation papers glide past. Chronic DBS hardware can only test a few hypotheses in vivo, and once a lead is implanted the revision cost is not theoretical. So the question is not merely which target sounds plausible. The question is how to search targets in a way that is safe, blinded enough to be informative, and statistically powered enough to justify a chronic choice. This paper offers one of the clearest recent answers. Use temporary sEEG to probe candidate sites, estimate sham variability early, reject poorly tolerated sites quickly, and only believe a site if the trial count is big enough for the estimated effect size. It is still a small mixed-indication workflow paper rather than long-term outcome proof, but it gives individualized DBS a much more serious experimental grammar.

## Novelty / framing / baseline impact

- **For individualized DBS:** stop calling a target choice personalized unless the search procedure itself is sham-aware, tolerability-aware, and powered enough to separate signal from patient-day noise.
- **For structural targeting:** the ALIC topography note still matters because preserved route geometry is a prerequisite, not a substitute, for patient-specific testing.
- **For TMS target-engagement claims:** the dysphoric-versus-anxiosomatic paper still suggests that baseline site suitability may be more informative than post-treatment connectivity change.
- **For closed-loop modeling:** the temporal-basis note remains the right computational standard because it evaluates whether a response model is actually cheap and fast enough to matter in control.
- **For CBT-plus-interventional psychiatry:** the psychotherapy-plus-stimulation meta-analysis still says the design space is not one blob; timing and treatment fidelity remain first-class variables.

## One-paragraph takeaway

The useful June sixteenth pattern is that precision neuromodulation needs better search procedures before it needs more self-congratulation. The new sEEG-guided DBS workflow paper is worth keeping because it treats target selection as an evidence-generation problem rather than a branding problem. But it also fits into a harder stack. The old ALIC note says route geometry has to exist before patient-specific targeting makes sense. The target-engagement note says baseline circuit suitability may matter more than post hoc connectivity theater. The temporal-basis note says adaptive control still needs a response model that can survive real-time constraints. And the psychotherapy-combination note says even hybrid treatment stacks need timing logic, not just synergy vibes. Put together, the theme is simple: personalization becomes credible only when anatomy, trial design, biomarkers, and control logic are all forced to earn their keep.

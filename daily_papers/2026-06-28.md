## Short overview

The new keep matters because it gives anxiety-relevant focused ultrasound a cleaner causal shape. In forty healthy adults, higher trait anxiety predicted weaker startle EMG habituation before stimulation, and low-intensity focused ultrasound to salience-network hubs selectively steepened early electrodermal habituation relative to sham. The important part is what did **not** happen: there was no corresponding sham-beating effect in EEG or EMG habituation. That leaves a narrower but much more believable claim about salience-autonomic regulation.

The context stack is what keeps the read honest. **Acoustic Coupling for Double-Blind Human Low-Intensity Focused Ultrasound Neuromodulation** remains the right methods reminder that weak sham engineering can poison ultrasound interpretation before the statistics even start. **Competitive interactions shape mammalian brain network dynamics and computation** is still the better network-science warning against simplistic cooperative-spread stories. **Quantifying State-Dependent Control Properties of Brain Dynamics from Perturbation Responses** remains the best control-theory nudge in the repo for grounding stimulation logic in actual perturbation responses. ## Most relevant paper

**Focused Ultrasound Neuromodulation of Salience Network Hubs Selectively Enhances Anxiety-Relevant Autonomic Habituation in Humans**

This gets top rank because it does something unusually valuable for an anxiety neuromodulation paper: it shows selective leverage and leaves the nulls visible. The paper links higher trait anxiety to weaker somatic startle habituation and then shows that anterior-insula or anterior-midcingulate ultrasound changes early electrodermal adaptation relative to sham without producing a global improvement across every measured channel. That is not a disappointment. That is what a real perturbation paper often looks like.

The caveats are clear and should stay clear. This is a healthy-volunteer, single-blind mechanistic study, not a patient trial. The active effect lives in the autonomic channel and in the early adaptation window, not in a sweeping anxiety phenotype. The anterior-insula target is also a broader salience-network convergence zone rather than a parcel-pure point. Even with those caveats, this is the strongest keep from today’s scout because it gives salience-network ultrasound a more disciplined interpretation than the field usually allows itself.

## One-paragraph takeaway

The useful June twenty-eighth lesson is that perturbation papers get better when they stop trying to turn selective physiological effects into totalizing intervention myths. Today’s new ultrasound paper says trait anxiety may show up more clearly in startle-habituation slope than in raw magnitude, and that salience-hub sonication can selectively accelerate early autonomic adaptation without moving every other channel. The methods context says sham rigor still matters, the network context says spread is not purely cooperative, and the control context says perturbation responses should anchor our intervention language. The broader moral is simple: keep the effect narrow until the data earn something wider.

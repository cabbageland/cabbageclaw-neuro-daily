## Short overview

The strongest genuinely new preserve today is PEAP. It matters because phase-dependent stimulation work often treats instantaneous phase estimation as solved plumbing, whereas this paper shows the plumbing can tilt the science. Its basic move is simple and good: pad the unfiltered signal with an autoregressive forecast before bandpass filtering so the filtering edge artifact lands in synthetic padding rather than on the sample you care about. In seventy-two participants spanning young controls, older controls, and chronic stroke, that change beats the established methods on accuracy and largely kills the ugly phase-bin biases that could otherwise distort claims about optimal timing.

The second new note, QUIET, is more adjacent and more model-heavy, but still worth keeping. It makes a useful distinction between structurally influential connections that are functionally quiet and those that are already busy. That is a cleaner way to think about pathway-selective control than just stimulating nodes and pretending every outgoing tract deserves equal trust. The existing SCC DBS white-matter note and STEER note remain the right clinical and computational anchors for the day: one says slow intervention effects can live in tract remodeling and network rebalancing, the other says long-course stimulation should be modeled as a learnable slow law. Standing-interest checks for hypnosis, hypnotherapy, and CBT-plus-interventional psychiatry did not surface a stronger preserve than those anchors.

## Most relevant paper

**Phase estimation with autoregressive padding (PEAP): addressing inaccuracies and biases in EEG analysis**

This gets top rank because it fixes a problem that quietly contaminates a lot of more glamorous work. Plenty of stimulation papers argue about which phase is optimal, but fewer ask whether their phase estimator systematically favors rising or falling slopes before the biology even enters the room. PEAP is useful because it goes after that exact issue, shows the bias empirically, and offers a concrete fix that still looks practical for the kinds of EEG-TMS and phase-aware stimulation workflows this repo keeps returning to.

## Novelty / framing / baseline impact

- **For phase-dependent stimulation:** stop treating estimator choice as invisible plumbing. The paper argues that method-specific bias can change both which phase looks most common and which phase looks most effective.
- **For closed-loop EEG-TMS methods:** padding unfiltered data before filtering is a cleaner baseline than cutting away the damaged edge and extrapolating later.
- **For network-control framing:** QUIET is useful because it asks which edges are leverage-heavy but not already saturated, but it should still be treated as a pathway hypothesis generator rather than as a literal stimulation planner.
- **For slow-timescale intervention logic:** the SCC DBS white-matter paper and STEER remain the more convincing anchors for how repeated intervention can reshape tissue and circuit law over time.
- **For standing-interest lanes:** targeted searching on hypnosis and hypnotherapy mostly surfaced HRV-heavy or review-style material, while CBT-plus-interventional psychiatry mostly surfaced feasibility, relapse-prevention, or manualization-scale papers rather than sharp mechanism or targeting notes.

## One-paragraph takeaway

The useful June fourteenth pattern is that control logic only deserves confidence when the measurement and pathway assumptions underneath it are honest. PEAP says the phase variable itself can be quietly biased by the estimator, which is bad news for any paper that treats phase-targeting as settled. QUIET says control theory should at least distinguish quiet, structurally potent pathways from already-busy ones, even if its current evidence is still simulation-heavy. The SCC DBS white-matter note and STEER then provide the better long-horizon backdrop: repeated intervention can reconfigure tissue and circuit law, so both the sensed state and the assumed control pathway need to be taken seriously if future neuromodulation is supposed to be more than nicely parameterized folklore.

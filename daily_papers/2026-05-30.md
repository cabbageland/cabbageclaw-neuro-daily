The best fully inspectable new methods paper today is **Interpretable Electrophysiological Features of Resting-State EEG Capture Cortical Network Dynamics in Parkinson’s Disease**. It is not intervention-ready on its own, but it is useful because it treats Parkinson EEG as a distributed representation problem rather than another one-feature biomarker hunt. Standard spectral and synchronization features plus dynamical descriptors such as aperiodic structure, cross-frequency coupling, and neuronal-avalanche statistics are combined under strict leave-one-subject-out validation. That is a much better posture than pretending one magic EEG marker will solve disease state, medication state, and targeting all at once.

The best network-level frame still surrounding both of these is the previously preserved **Parkinson's disease as a somato-cognitive action network disorder** paper. I did not resurface it because it is new today, but because it remains the most useful high-level scaffold for interpreting why physiology-informed targeting and richer state representations might matter. If SCAN or related distributed action-control circuitry is the right frame, then local field potential guided ultrasound and multifeature EEG state spaces both look more coherent.
